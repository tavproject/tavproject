About US

“Team Against Violence” (TAV)  project’s mission is to advocate and centralize social awareness of sexual violence. TAV recognizes that violence takes many forms, including but not limited to, bullying, harassment, rape, sexual assault, -intimate partner violence, child and elder abuse and other form of illicit act intended to cause someone’s life. We exist to promote prevention strategies. We are to inform, educate, and improve societal response to either racial, gender, color, religion or other type of discrimination against a person that can transpire in various form in any places i.e. work, community, education, laws, etc. “No one shall be deprived of life liberty.”

  • TAV was introduced by a group of aspiring volunteers in July 2020.
  • TAV is fully controlled and operated by Volunteers.
  • TAV receives no governmental funds nor has any outsource income.
  • TAV funding source is through our sponsors help us to assure the success of our mission.

It offers experiential workshop in personal growth, community development, aimed to help transform survivors of sexual violence and improve societal response to sexual violence.

Statistics on Sexual & Domestic Violence

  • 1 in 5 women and 1 in 16 men will be sexually assaulted while in college.
  • More than 90% of sexual assault victims on college campuses do not report the assault.
  • More than 50% college sexual assaults occur August-November
  • According to the National Institute of Justice, rape survivors knew their attacker as a
    • Classmate/fellow student: 25.9%
    • Friend: 34.1%
    • Boyfriend or ex-boyfriend: 27.4%
    • Acquaintance: 39.2%.
  • 43% of dating college women report experiencing violent and abusive dating behaviors (physical, sexual, technology-facilitated, verbal or other controlling behavior).
  • 13% of female college students have been stalked at school.
