Job Description

A community relations manager sustains positive relationships with community members, nonprofit organizations, and fellow employees in order to cultivate a favorable operating climate for their business or organization. They carefully develop a strategy designed to make positive contributions to a local area while raising awareness for their company. People with strong presentation skills and a desire to interact with a variety of groups can succeed in this role.

Responsibilities

  • While a community relations manager’s day-to-day duties and responsibilities are determined by where they work, there are many core tasks associated with the role. Based on our analysis of job listings, these include:

    Serve as a Key Point of Contact

    Community relations managers act a key point of contact for individual residents, business leaders, and customers in a local area. Their responsibility is to kick off relationships and support opportunities. They effectively communicate with individuals at various levels to discover the issues affecting them and address pressing concerns.

    Plan Community Relations Programs

    It is the job of a community relations manager to brainstorm, plan, organize, and manage activities that help their organization achieve its pubic relations goals. They execute events or implement programs closely aligned with corporate strategy. Community relations managers support business goals by raising community awareness of available products or services.

    Manage Support Staff

    Some community relations managers supervise the day-to-day operations of junior community relations staff. They ensure that departmental goals and milestones are met, handle personnel issues, and improve department process. Through direct and indirect supervision, they leverage their team to create positive PR results on behalf of their company.

    Build Strong Community Relationships

    Developing strong relationships with industries, communities, government, and media is a key responsibility of community relations managers. By interfacing with vital community groups, they ensure that their organization produces effective results. They also use these outside partnerships to increase the reach of organizational initiatives and programs.

    Create Marketing Materials

    Community relations managers help prepare a variety of publicity materials. They write news releases, company stories, factsheets, talking points, speeches, and social media content. They make sure that any print or digital materials offered to the community enhance their organization’s public image.

  • Great interpersonal and communication skills
  • Community relations managers are organized individuals with outstanding people skills and a professional attitude.
